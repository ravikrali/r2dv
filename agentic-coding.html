Executive Summary

The Structural Crisis in Enterprise Transformation

Organizations invest $50M to $1B+ in multi-year transformation initiatives to modernize legacy systems, integrate SaaS platforms, and migrate to cloud infrastructure — yet 70% fail to deliver expected business value, run significantly over budget, or extend far beyond planned timelines.

The root cause is not poor project management. It is the fundamental mismatch between the pace of business change and the capacity constraints of human-driven software delivery. Traditional IT delivery models remain bottlenecked by manual coding, sequential testing cycles, point-in-time documentation, and architectural governance that occurs too late to prevent costly rework.

Agentic coding represents an inflection point. These are not incremental productivity improvements — they are autonomous AI agents that independently execute complex software engineering tasks: analyzing codebases across repositories, generating production-quality code with full test coverage, validating changes against architectural standards, and maintaining living documentation — all without continuous human direction.

Future Workforce Model

Agentic coding does not eliminate IT roles — it fundamentally redefines them. Roles centered on manual execution diminish. Roles requiring strategic thinking, architectural judgment, and organizational change leadership expand significantly.
